# Happy to Have Lived — Privacy Policy

*Last updated: April 1, 2026*

## Summary

Happy to Have Lived does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. All data stays on your device.

## What the app accesses

**Health data (HealthKit)** — Used to import workouts and health metrics into your local goal-tracking database. Health data is read locally on your device. It is never sent to a server, shared with third parties, or stored outside the app's local database. You control which health data types the app can access through iOS Settings.

**On-device language model (Apple Intelligence)** — Used to generate personalized greetings. All language processing happens locally on your device using Apple's Foundation Models framework. No text is transmitted to any server.

## What the app does not do

- No user accounts or sign-in
- No cloud sync (iCloud sync is not active)
- No analytics or crash reporting
- No third-party SDKs that collect data
- No advertising
- No data shared with third parties
- No data transmitted to any server

## Data stored on your device

Happy to Have Lived stores your goals, milestones, commitments, progress entries, and imported health data in a local SQLite database on your device. This data is not accessible to other apps and is removed when you delete the app.

JSON backup and restore is available for personal use — backups are created and stored locally by you.
